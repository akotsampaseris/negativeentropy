My Story

Early beginnings

I grew up in a small seaside town in rural Greece, Nea Artaki, mostly playing football in the streets and working with computers, taking them apart and putting them back together (most of the time)... I wasn't a big fan of school, always found that it suppressed creativity and intelligence, and rewarded conformity and homogeneity. My passion at the time was football and I wanted to become a football manager, so I entered the School of Sports Science and Physical Education in the University of Athens. During my studies in Athens, I unexpectedly fell in love with Physics and Mathematics and I decided to move to the UK and become a scientist.

Living in England

Life in the north of England was very difficult in the beginning, with the constant rain and the grey skies. For years I was solely focused on my work, and as a result I finished in the top place in every single year of my education. During the second year of my Bachelor's degree, I was working with Dr. James Christian and Dr. Graham McDonald on Fresnel diffraction patterns from fractal apertures, and I wrote a computer program that made our calculations 400 times faster and accelerated the analysis of the results, which were presented in the European Optical Society Annual Meeting (EOSAM 2016) in Berlin. I continued working with Dr. Christian and Dr. McDonald and what started as my Final Project became a series of papers on Spatiotemporal Solitons that were published in the Physical Review A of the Americal Physical Society. Then I took a big chance and decided to work on Particle Physics and I started my Master's degree on Particle and Mathematical Physics in the University of Durham, where I had an opportunity to work with Prof Simon Ross on the AdS/CFT Correspondence and the Holographic Entanglement Entropy in AdS Spacetimes, where I received the Winton MSc Prize for the top place in the Master's.

Next Chapter

Soon I realised that the academic path was not the best choice for me. I came back to Greece and I started working as a Junior Developer for TechnoLogic LTD, in order to gain real-life work experience and learn how to code professionally, which was much more different from coding on your own leisure. But then I was offered an opportunity to start a PhD in Quantum Plasmonics in the University of Birmingham, and I returned to England for a few months. However, I kept on working remotely for TechnoLogic LTD, and during that time I was convinced that a career in Programming was a much better choice for me. I am now back in Greece and working to continuously evolve as a programmer and one day to become an entrepreneur.
